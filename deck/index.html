The gap

A $2B+/yr market has never had a price you can trust onchain.

  • Graded cards trade across a dozen unconnected venues — eBay, PWCC, Heritage, Goldin, ALT, Courtyard. No two agree, and no one is neutral.
  • Markets are thin. Many cards see 0–2 sales a month. A single wash trade ($200–$3K in fees) can move the “price” at will.
  • Existing feeds are point-in-time, single-source, and unauditable. You take the number on faith.
The consequence

No trustworthy price → no onchain market.

Prediction markets, lending, fractionalization, insurance — every RWA primitive for collectibles is blocked on the same missing piece: a credibly-neutral, manipulation-resistant valuation that anyone can verify.

Ormer-style median estimators measure ~75% better manipulation-resistance than naïve mean/TWAP in thin markets.

The thesis

The market is the showcase.
The oracle is the product.

Anyone can trade YES/NO on whether a card beats a strike by expiry. To settle, the market needs a real price. That price isn’t a feed — it’s a swarm of source-specialist agents that read every major venue, remember each card’s history and every manipulation attempt across sessions, reconcile to a manipulation-resistant consensus, and publish the evidence so anyone can verify it.

MemWal · the memory that makes it smart

Cold-start once. Smarter every run after.

  • Per-card price history — last 100 observations per source, so today’s number is judged against the card’s own past, not a vacuum.
  • Source reliability weights — an EMA reputation score per agent (0.7·hit-rate + 0.3·prior). Sources that track consensus earn weight; those that drift lose it.
  • Manipulation patterns — every flagged anomaly (price jumps, stale feeds, seller concentration) is remembered per card.

The hard part · manipulation resistance

Thin markets are easy to spoof. We make it expensive and pointless.

Per-agent

Circuit breakers

Reject zero-price; flag >5× / <0.2× jumps, >14-day stale feeds, and >60% single-seller concentration.

Coordinator

MAD outlier rejection

Modified Z-score (threshold 3.5) drops comps that don’t agree with the robust median.

Coordinator

Confidence-weighted median

Pyth-style: weight = confidence × reliability × recency. A wash trade can’t outvote the crowd.

Onchain

Source gate + dispute

Refuse to settle under 3 independent sources; UMA-style bonded dispute window for high-value cards.

Wide-disagreement breaker trips when the 25–75% interval exceeds 40% of consensus → the oracle declines to settle rather than settle wrong.

Don’t trust — verify

Every price ships with its receipts.

On each consensus the coordinator emits an evidence bundle — consensus price, every contributing comp, per-source weights, rejected outliers, the exact aggregation method — and stores it on Walrus. The blob ID is referenced onchain at resolution.

Anyone can pull the blob and re-run the math. The oracle is auditable by construction, not by promise.
