The problem

Graded cards trade across a dozen venues. No two agree.

  • Thin markets — many cards see 0–2 sales a month. A single wash trade can move the "price."
  • Fragmented data — eBay, Goldin, ALT, Courtyard, PriceCharting, TCGPlayer. Each has part of the picture, none is neutral.
  • No onchain price → no onchain market. Prediction markets, lending, fractionalization — every collectible RWA primitive is blocked.
The consequence

Every feed is single-source, point-in-time, and unauditable.

You take the number on faith. There's no way to verify how a price was computed, what was excluded, or who benefits from the answer.

The thesis

The market is the showcase.
The oracle is the product.

Anyone can trade YES/NO on whether a card exceeds a strike price by expiry. To settle, the market needs a real price — not a feed, but a swarm of agents that read every major venue, remember each card's history, and publish evidence on Walrus so anyone can verify.

MemWal · the memory layer

Cold-start once. Smarter every run. Memory lives on Walrus.

  • Per-card price history — last 100 observations per source. Today's number judged against the card's own past.
  • Reputation weights — EMA reliability per agent. Sources that track consensus earn weight; drifters lose it.
  • Anomaly memory — every flagged manipulation (price jumps, seller concentration) remembered per card.
  • Walrus persistence — full memory snapshots to Walrus after every run. Kill the process, restore from a blob ID. The swarm outlives its operator.

Manipulation resistance

Thin markets are easy to spoof. We make it expensive and pointless.

Per-agent

Circuit breakers

Reject zero-price, flag >5× jumps, stale feeds >14d, >60% single-seller concentration.

Coordinator

MAD outlier rejection

Modified Z-score (threshold 3.5) drops signals that diverge from the robust median.

Coordinator

Weighted median

Weight = confidence × reliability × recency. A wash trade can't outvote the crowd.

Onchain

Source gate + dispute

Won't settle under 3 sources. UMA-style bonded dispute window. Wide disagreement → oracle declines.

Spoofing one source costs ~$6K in marketplace fees and gets MAD-rejected. Coordinating 4+ sources costs $23K+ and still gets caught by reputation + drift detection.

Don't trust — verify

Every price ships with its receipts on Walrus.

Each consensus round emits an evidence bundle — price, every comp, per-source weights, rejected outliers, the aggregation method — and stores it on Walrus. The blob ID is referenced onchain. A market cannot settle without it.

Anyone pulls the blob and re-runs the math. The oracle is auditable by construction.
