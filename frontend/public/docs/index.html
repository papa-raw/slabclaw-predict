Anima Swarm

Command AI spirits through whispers—memories persist on Walrus.

Anima Swarm is a 6-player deity-controlled AI strategy game on a hex grid. You don't move units directly—you issue decrees to your swarm and whisper to enemy swarms. Spirits interpret your commands through their personality, bond level, and accumulated memories. Every whisper, battle, spawn, and territory claim is stored as verifiable memory on Walrus via MemWal.

The Whisper System

You command your swarm through whispers—strategic messages sent on a cooldown. Each player gets 2 whispers per 30-second cycle: one for your own swarm, one to target an enemy swarm.

Swarm Decree

A broadcast to all your living spirits. Every spirit receives the decree, stores it as a [DEITY DECREE] memory on MemWal, and interprets it through their personality. High-bond spirits follow orders faithfully; low-bond spirits may twist the meaning.

CHOSEN BY GOD

If you mention a spirit by name in your decree, that spirit gets divine attention—a temporary +10 bond boost and heightened confidence in their next action. Use this to target specific spirits for critical missions.

Enemy Whisper

Choose one enemy player's swarm and send a message to all their spirits. The effect depends on each spirit's enemy resistance stat:

ResistanceEffectLoyalty Impact
70–100Ignored—memory stored but no behavioral effectNone
40–69Eroded—spirit hears you but resists−2
10–39Shaken—may override deity orders (40% chance)−5
0–9Defecting—deity orders fully replaced−8

Each enemy whisper received reduces the target spirit's resistance by 1. Over time, patient deities can weaken an opponent's swarm from within.

Resistance Drift

  • High loyalty (≥75) spirits slowly recover resistance (+0.2/tick, cap 80)
  • Low loyalty (<25) spirits slowly lose resistance (−0.1/tick, floor 10)

Whisper Charges

CHARGE ECONOMY

1 swarm decree + 1 enemy whisper per 30-second cycle. Use-it-or-lose-it—charges don't stack. The WhisperBar at the bottom of the screen shows your charge state and a countdown to the next reset.

Soul Mining

Soul deposits appear randomly across the hex grid as glowing teal particles. They represent ambient spiritual energy that gatherer spirits can harvest. On the map, soul-rich hexes glow with pulsing teal particles—more particles = more energy available.

THE SPAWNING ECONOMY

Soul mining is the economic engine of expansion. Gatherers harvest soul energy → stored as spirit memories → 10 memories consumed per spawn → new spirits born. Without gatherers mining soul deposits, your swarm cannot grow.

  • ~10% of land hexes receive new soul deposits each tick
  • Deposits range from 2–5 energy per spawn event
  • Gatherers absorb up to 10 energy per soul mine action (instant)
  • Each unit of soul energy = 1 memory + 1 Social XP for the gatherer
  • Hexes cap at 60 soul energy

Combat

Battles are LLM-arbitrated. When two opposing spirits occupy the same hex, either can initiate combat. The arbiter evaluates:

  • Bond score (40%)—deity connection strength
  • Tactical score (35%)—specialization advantage, terrain, XP
  • Narrative score (25%)—personality-driven invocation quality

Outcomes: close margin = loser retreats to an adjacent hex. Wide margin (≥8 points) = loser dies. If the loser is surrounded with no retreat path, they also die. Winners claim the hex.

Spawning & Lineage

Spirits can spawn children when they have 10+ memories and 35+ average bond. Spawning consumes 10 memories from the parent—making soul mining essential for swarm growth.

The child inherits the parent's most formative memories (LLM-ranked) and develops its own personality. Children start as generalists with 30% of the parent's bond stats.

Spirits track their lineage: generation number, parent, past life names (if reincarnated via essence import), and memorable actions.

Territory & Win Conditions

Hexes are claimed by the player whose spirit occupies them. Spirits heading toward a hex show a pulsing highlight on their target destination.

The game ends when:

  • A player controls 50% of all hexes, or
  • Only one player has living spirits, or
  • The max tick count (500 ticks) is reached—most territory wins

Enemy Diplomacy

Enemy spirits have an enemy resistance stat (0–100) that determines how susceptible they are to your whispers. Use the Enemy Whisper charge to target an opponent's entire swarm. The more you whisper, the lower their resistance drops—eventually allowing you to override their deity's orders or even trigger defections.

This is a long game: each whisper only drops resistance by 1, but loyal spirits recover slowly while disloyal ones decay further. Focus your whispers on the weakest-loyalty spirits for maximum effect.

Essence & Reincarnation

After a game ends, you can export your swarm's essence—a snapshot of memories, personality, lineage, and battle history—as a Walrus blob. In the next game's lobby, import that blob ID to reincarnate your spirits with their past-life memories intact. This is how spirits persist across games.
