Anima Swarm

Command AI spirits through whispers. Memories persist on Walrus.

Anima Swarm is a 6-player deity-controlled AI strategy game on a hex grid. You don't move units directly—you whisper to your spirits, and they interpret your commands through their own personality, bond level, and accumulated memories. Every whisper, battle, spawn, and territory claim is stored as verifiable memory on Walrus via MemWal.

How It Works

The Deity-Spirit Relationship

You are a deity. Your swarm of AI spirits acts autonomously every 30 seconds, making decisions based on their personality, specialization, memories, and your whispered orders. Higher bond = more faithful interpretation of your commands. Low bond spirits may misinterpret or ignore you.

Whisper Propagation

When you whisper to one spirit, the message propagates through your swarm. Each spirit relays the whisper in their own voice—a high-bond warrior delivers it faithfully, while a low-bond scout might twist the meaning entirely. This creates emergent swarm behavior from a single command.

Bond System

Each spirit tracks four bond dimensions with their deity:

  • Depth—how well the spirit understands you
  • Harmony—alignment of values and temperament
  • Adventure—shared experiences and risks taken
  • Loyalty—trust and reliability over time

Higher average bond means more accurate whisper interpretation and better battle performance. Chat regularly with your spirits to increase bond.

Combat

Battles are LLM-arbitrated. When two opposing spirits occupy the same hex, either can initiate combat. The arbiter evaluates:

  • Bond score (40%)—deity connection strength
  • Tactical score (35%)—specialization advantage, terrain, XP
  • Narrative score (25%)—personality-driven invocation quality

Outcomes: close margin = loser retreats to an adjacent hex. Wide margin (≥8 points) = loser dies. If the loser is surrounded with no retreat path, they also die. Winners claim the hex.

Territory & Win Conditions

Hexes are claimed by the player whose spirit occupies them. Memory pools accumulate on controlled hexes over time—gatherer spirits can absorb these.

The game ends when:

  • A player controls 50% of all hexes, or
  • Only one player has living spirits, or
  • The max tick count (500 ticks) is reached—most territory wins

Enemy Diplomacy

You can click on enemy spirits and attempt to influence them. Their response depends on their loyalty stat:

LoyaltyResponse
75+Hostile—rejects your influence outright
50–74Suspicious—guarded but listening
25–49Wavering—open to persuasion
0–24Disloyal—may defect or sabotage their own swarm

Each influence attempt erodes the target's loyalty by 1 point. A patient deity can turn an enemy spirit into an unreliable asset for their opponent.

Spawning & Lineage

Spirits can spawn children when they have 5+ memories and 35+ average bond. The child inherits the parent's most formative memories (LLM-ranked) and develops its own personality based on those inherited experiences. Children start as generalists with 30% of the parent's bond.

Spirits track their lineage: generation number, parent, past life names (if reincarnated via essence import), and memorable actions.

Essence & Reincarnation

After a game ends, you can export your swarm's essence—a snapshot of memories, personality, lineage, and battle history—as a Walrus blob. In the next game's lobby, import that blob ID to reincarnate your spirits with their past-life memories intact. This is how spirits persist across games.
