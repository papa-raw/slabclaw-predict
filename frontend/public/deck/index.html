The problem

Graded cards trade across a dozen venues. No two report the same price.

  • Thin markets: many cards see 0–2 sales a month, so one wash trade can set the "price."
  • No shared method: eBay is sold comps, Goldin is auction hammers, ALT is an index, TCGPlayer is live asks — different methods, grades, and recency, and every venue has a stake in its own number.
  • No onchain price → no onchain market: prediction markets, lending, and fractionalization are all blocked without one.
The consequence

Every feed is single-source, point-in-time, and unauditable.

You take the number on faith. There's no way to verify how a price was computed, what was excluded, or who benefits from the answer.

The thesis

The market is the showcase.
The oracle swarm that remembers is the product.

Anyone trades YES/NO on whether a card beats a strike price by expiry. To settle, the market needs a real price — not a feed, but a swarm of 9 agents that read every major venue, remember each card's history, and publish evidence on Walrus so anyone can verify.

Architecture

Three tiers: source → consensus → chain

MemWal · the memory layer

Cold-start once. Smarter every run.

  • Per-card price history: the last 100 observations per source, so today's number is judged against the card's own past.
  • Reputation weights: a running average of how often each source has agreed with the final consensus. Sources that track it earn weight; drifters lose it.
  • Anomaly memory: every flagged manipulation (price jumps, seller concentration) is remembered per card and pre-filtered next run.
  • Walrus persistence: the whole learned state snapshots to Walrus each run. A fresh machine restores it from one blob ID — no warm-up, no central database, and the memory can't be silently altered.

Observable learning · the Walrus payoff

Not just stored on Walrus. Smarter because of it.

Memory persisted to Walrus is what lets the swarm improve run over run — and you can watch it happen.

Reliability divergence

Sources earn their weight

Everyone starts at 1.0. The reputation EMA learns who tracks consensus: eBay · PriceCharting · Courtyard hold ~86%; thin or ask-only sources settle to ~44–49%.

Confidence narrowing

Bands tighten with data

Each independent realized source narrows the consensus interval — and as a card's comp history, source reputations, and calibration all sharpen together, the band tightens and the settle gets more reliable.

Self-correcting flags

It learns each card's norm

The quality tier stores a per-card calibration on Walrus. As a card warms, false-positive manipulation flags fall — the swarm stops crying wolf on that card's real spread.

Kill the process, restore memory from a Walrus blob, and the learned weights + calibrations come back with it. Persistence is the mechanism, not the feature.

Manipulation resistance

Thin markets are easy to spoof. We make it expensive and pointless.

Per-agent

Circuit breakers

Reject zero-price, flag >5× jumps, stale feeds >14d, >60% single-seller concentration.

Coordinator

MAD outlier rejection

Modified Z-score (threshold 3.5) drops signals that diverge from the robust median.

Coordinator

Weighted median

Weight = confidence × reliability × recency. A wash trade can't outvote the crowd.

Onchain

Source gate + dispute

Won't settle under 3 sources. UMA-style bonded dispute window. Wide disagreement → oracle declines.

One wash source is 1 of 9 — MAD drops it. Moving the median takes faking many independent marketplaces at once, and any fresh source starts at low reputation weight, so it barely counts.

Don't trust — verify

Every price ships with its receipts on Walrus.

Each consensus round emits an evidence bundle — price, every comp, per-source weights, rejected outliers, the aggregation method — stored on Walrus with the blob ID referenced onchain. A market cannot settle without it; anyone pulls the blob and re-runs the math. Auditable by construction.

  • Live on Sui testnet: 4 PSA-10 markets with faucet tUSD.
  • Every resolution carries a verifiable evidence_blob_id onchain.
